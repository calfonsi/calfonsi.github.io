Troubleshooting Basics

When a subscriber reports an issue to Customer Care, the first step is to identify the correct issue type (Billing, Content, or Tech) so you can determine the appropriate next actions.

  • Billing: Issues related to payment processing, refund errors, entitlement access, whitelisting, or billing failures.
  • Content: Missing episodes, incorrect availability, wrong-title playback, or other content-related errors.
  • Tech: Problems affecting playback, app performance, logins, or streaming functionality.

Once the issue type is confirmed, Customer Care should complete three core tasks in their investigation:

  • Perform basic troubleshooting steps.
  • Test or replicate the issue when possible. If testing is not possible, collect screenshots or video from the subscriber.
  • Collect key information from the subscriber, including ruling out potential blockers (e.g., a VPN for tech issues or a chargeback for billing issues).

All must be completed fully before escalating the ticket further.

⚠️ Attention: The BCT articles (Escalation Workflows, Data Gathering Guide, Troubleshooting Steps, and Testing) do not follow a fixed sequence. Customer Care should start with the article that best fits the situation, as the order may change based on the subscriber’s concern. These resources work together and should be applied thoughtfully for each case.

Common Troubleshooting Steps

The action steps below cover the most common troubleshooting steps to complete with a subscriber when they report an issue, but they are not a complete list of every possible fix or supported device.

If you encounter a device or version not listed here, search online for the specific action and device name (for example, 'clear cache Samsung Smart TV') to guide the subscriber accurately. Always verify that the instructions you share align with BritBox’s supported platforms and current app behavior.

💡 Tip: Not all of these actions need to be performed for every issue, but many of them may apply.

Clearing Cache and Cookies (Web Browsers)

Cached data and cookies help websites load faster, but over time, they can store outdated information that interferes with updates or playback. Clearing them can resolve login, playback, or loading issues.

If the subscriber recently experienced errors after a site update, clearing cache, and cookies is often the first troubleshooting step.

Below are the steps to clear the cache and cookies in different browsers on various devices.

Instructions:

Google Chrome (Computer)

Option 1 – Clear all cookies and cache

  1. Open Chrome
  2. In the top-right corner, click 'More (⋮)'
  3. Select 'Settings' > 'Privacy and security' > 'Clear browsing data'
  4. Choose a time range (for example, All time)
  5. Select 'Cookies and other site data' and 'Cached images and files'
  6. Click 'Clear data'

Clearing cookies signs the user out of most websites and may remove saved preferences.

Option 2 – Clear data for a specific site

  1. Open Chrome and go to 'Settings' > 'Privacy and security' > 'Third-party cookies'
  2. Select 'See all site data and permissions'
  3. Search for BritBox.com in the top-right search bar
  4. Click 'Delete' next to the site name, then confirm by clicking 'Delete' again

Google Chrome (Android)

  1. Open Chrome
  2. Tap 'More (⋮)' > 'Clear browsing data'
  3. Choose a time range or tap 'All time'
  4. Select 'Cookies and site data' and 'Cached images and files'
  5. Tap 'Clear data'

Google Chrome (iPhone / iPad)

  1. Open Chrome
  2. Tap 'More (⋮)' > 'Clear browsing data'
  3. Select the types of data to remove
  4. Tap 'Clear browsing data,' then tap 'Done'

Mozilla Firefox (Computer)

Option 1 – Clear all data

  1. Click the 'Menu (☰)' button in the top-right corner
  2. Go to 'Settings' > 'Privacy & Security'
  3. Scroll to 'Cookies and Site Data'
  4. Click 'Clear Data,' select both checkboxes, and click 'Clear Now'

Option 2 – Clear data for BritBox only

  1. While on the BritBox website, click the padlock icon next to the URL
  2. Select 'Clear Cookies and Site Data'
  3. Click 'Remove' to confirm

Microsoft Edge (Computer)

Option 1 – Clear all cookies and cache

  1. Open Edge
  2. Click 'More (⋯)' in the upper-right corner > 'Settings'
  3. Select 'Privacy, search, and services'
  4. Under Clear browsing data, click Choose what to clear
  5. Choose a time range
  6. Select 'Cookies and other site data,' then click 'Clear now'

Option 2 – Clear data for BritBox only

  1. Open Edge > 'Settings' > 'Cookies and site permissions'
  2. Select 'Manage' and delete cookies and site data > 'See all cookies and site data'
  3. Search for BritBox.com, expand the entry, and select 'Delete'

Apple Safari (Computer)

  1. Open Safari
  2. In the menu bar, click Safari > 'Settings' > 'Privacy'
  3. Click 'Manage Website Data,' then 'Remove All' or search for BritBox.com and click 'Remove'

Apple Safari (iPhone / iPad)

  1. Open 'Settings' on the device
  2. Scroll down and tap 'Safari'
  3. Tap 'Clear History and Website Data,' then confirm

Updating Web Browsers

Keeping browsers up to date helps prevent playback issues, improves security, and ensures compatibility with the latest BritBox features.

If a subscriber experiences freezing, blank pages or playback errors, check that their browser is updated to the latest version.

Below are the steps to update web browsers on different devices.

Instructions:

Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox and Microsoft Edge (Windows)

  1. Open the browser
  2. Click the 'Menu (⋮ or ☰)' icon in the top-right corner
  3. Select 'Help' > 'About [Browser Name]'
  4. The browser will automatically check for updates and install any available version
  5. When prompted, click 'Relaunch' or 'Restart' to complete the update

Apple Safari (Mac)

  1. Click the Apple menu in the top-left corner
  2. Select 'System Settings' (or System Preferences on older macOS versions)
  3. Go to 'General' > 'Software Update'
  4. If a macOS update is available, click 'Update Now.' Safari updates automatically through macOS updates

Linux Systems

Linux browsers can be updated through the system’s package manager or directly within the browser.
Encourage the subscriber to check their distribution’s documentation for specific steps.

Microsoft Edge may not be available on all Linux distributions.

Updating BritBox App

Keeping the BritBox app updated ensures access to the latest features, performance improvements, and bug fixes.

If a subscriber reports playback issues or missing features, confirm they are using the most recent version of the app.

Below are the steps to update the BritBox app on different devices.

Instructions:

Android Devices

  1. Open the Google Play Store
  2. Tap the Profile icon in the top-right corner
  3. Select 'Manage apps & device'
  4. Under 'Updates available,' look for 'BritBox'
  5. Tap 'Update'

If BritBox doesn’t appear, the app is already up to date.

Apple iPhone and iPad

  1. Open the App Store
  2. Tap the Profile icon in the top-right corner
  3. Scroll down to see pending updates
  4. If BritBox appears, tap 'Update'

Roku

  1. Highlight the BritBox channel on the Home screen
  2. Press the 'Star (*)' button on the remote
  3. Select 'Check for updates'
  4. If an update is available, Roku will install it automatically

Fire TV

  1. Highlight the BritBox app on the Home screen
  2. Press the 'Menu (☰)' button on the remote
  3. Select 'More Info'
  4. If an update is available, choose 'Update'

Apple TV

  1. Open 'Settings' > 'Apps'
  2. Ensure 'Automatically Update Apps' is turned on
  3. To manually update, open the App Store, find BritBox and select 'Update' if available

Samsung Smart TVs

  1. Press the 'Home' button on the remote
  2. Select 'Apps,' then the Settings icon
  3. Highlight BritBox
  4. Select 'Update' if available

LG Smart TVs

  1. Open the LG Content Store
  2. Select 'Apps' > 'My Apps'
  3. Highlight BritBox and select 'Update'

Fetch

  1. Press 'Menu' > 'Manage' > 'Apps'
  2. Highlight BritBox and select 'Update App' if available

Force Stopping an Application

Force stopping closes an app that may be frozen, unresponsive, or running in the background. This is often one of the first steps when the BritBox app fails to load, freezes, or won’t close properly.

Below are the steps to force stop an application on different devices.

Instructions:

Android Devices

Method 1 – Through Settings

  1. Open the Settings app
  2. Tap 'Apps' or 'Apps & notifications'
  3. Find and select BritBox from the list
  4. Tap 'Force Stop,' then confirm

Method 2 – From the App Icon (Android 7 or newer)

  1. Long-press the BritBox app icon
  2. Select 'App info'
  3. Tap 'Force Stop,' then confirm

After force stopping, you can also clear the app’s cache and data if issues persist.

Fire TV Devices

  1. From the Home screen, go to 'Settings' > 'Applications' > 'Manage Installed Applications'
  2. Select BritBox
  3. Choose 'Force Stop'

If the problem continues, clear the app’s cache and data in the same menu.

Apple TV

  1. Press the TV button on the Siri Remote twice to open the App Switcher
  2. Swipe left or right to locate BritBox
  3. Swipe up on the remote’s touch surface to close the app

iPhone and iPad

  1. Swipe up from the bottom of the screen and pause in the middle (or double-click the 'Home' button on older models)
  2. Swipe left or right to locate BritBox
  3. Swipe up on the app preview to close it

Clearing App Cache and Data (Android and Fire TV)

Clearing an app’s cache and data removes temporary files that may interfere with playback, logins, or app performance. This step often resolves errors such as the app freezing, crashing, or showing outdated information.

Clearing data will sign the subscriber out of their BritBox account.

Below are the steps to clear the app cache and data on Android and Fire TV.

Below are the steps to update the BritBox app on different devices.

Instructions:

Android Devices

Method 1 – Through Settings

  1. Open the Settings app
  2. Tap 'Apps' or 'Apps & notifications'
  3. Find and select BritBox
  4. Tap 'Storage & cache'
  5. Tap 'Clear Cache,' then 'Clear Storage or Clear Data'

Method 2 – From the App Icon (Android 7 or newer)

  1. Long-press the BritBox app icon
  2. Select 'App info'
  3. Tap 'Storage & cache'
  4. Tap 'Clear Cache,' then 'Clear Storage or Clear Data'

Clear cache first, then data if the issue persists. This helps remove old files without immediately signing the user out.

Fire TV Devices

  1. From the Home screen, go to 'Settings' > 'Applications' > 'Manage Installed Applications'
  2. Select BritBox
  3. Choose 'Clear Cache,' then 'Clear Data'

Always clear the cache first, then the data. Clearing data removes stored account information, so the user will need to sign in again.

After clearing cache and data: Reopen the BritBox app, sign back in and test playback. If issues continue, restart the device before trying again.

Rebooting or Restarting Devices

Restarting a device can resolve temporary software issues and clear background processes that may interfere with app performance. This should be done after major updates, cache clears, or whenever the BritBox app becomes unresponsive.

Below are the steps to reboot or restart different devices.

Instructions:

Android Devices

  1. Hold the Power button until the Power menu appears
  2. Tap 'Restart' or 'Reboot'
  3. Wait for the device to fully power back on before reopening BritBox

Apple iPhone and iPad

  1. Hold the Power button and either Volume button until the 'Slide to Power Off' slider appears
  2. Drag the slider to turn the device off
  3. Wait a few seconds, then hold the Power button again until the Apple logo appears

Roku

  1. Press the 'Home' button on the Roku remote
  2. Go to 'Settings' > 'System' > 'System Restart'
  3. Select Restart and wait for the device to reboot

For Roku TVs:
Go to 'Settings' > 'System' > 'Power' > 'System Restart' > 'Restart'

Fire TV

  1. From the Home screen, go to 'Settings' > 'My Fire TV' > 'Restart'
  2. Confirm when prompted
  3. Wait for the device to power back on before reopening BritBox

Apple TV

  1. Open the Settings app
  2. Go to 'System' > 'Restart'
  3. Confirm and wait for the device to reboot

Samsung Smart TV

  1. Hold the 'Power' button on the Samsung remote until the TV turns off and back on
  2. Wait for the Samsung logo to appear before releasing the button

Releasing the button too early will only put the TV in standby mode.

LG Smart TV

  1. Hold the 'Volume Down' and 'Power' buttons on the remote at the same time
  2. Continue holding until the TV powers off and the LG logo appears
  3. Release both buttons when the logo displays

Fetch

  1. On the remote, press 'Menu' > 'Manage' > 'Settings' > 'Device Info' > 'Restart Fetch Box'
  2. Confirm and wait for the box to restart

Always confirm the BritBox app version after a reboot. A restart often triggers updates or clears lingering background errors.

Running Speed and Network Tests

Internet speed and network stability directly affect streaming quality. Running a quick connection test helps determine if playback issues are caused by slow speeds or weak signal strength.

Speed tests should be done on the same device that is experiencing the issue to get the most accurate results.

Below are the steps to run speed and network tests on various devices.

Below are the steps to update the BritBox app on different devices.

Instructions:

All Web Browsers (Computer, Phones and Tablets)

  1. Open a web browser on the affected device
  2. Go to one of the following sites:
    • www.speedtest.net
    • www.fast.com
  3. Click 'Go' or 'Start Test'
  4. Note the download speed and ping/latency results

Google Search Option

  1. Go to Google.com
  2. Type speed test in the search bar
  3. Click 'Run Speed Test' or 'Start Speed Test'

This option may not be available on all mobile or Smart TV browsers.

Roku Devices

  1. Press the 'Home' button on the Roku remote
  2. Go to 'Settings' > 'Network' > 'Check Connection'
  3. After the test, select 'About' to view connection details
    • Network Connection Strength: shows signal quality to the router
    • Download Speed: shows estimated streaming speed

Samsung Smart TVs

  1. On the remote, press 'Settings'
  2. Go to 'General' > 'Network' > 'Network Status to test the internet connection'
  3. For additional testing, go to 'Support' > 'Self Diagnosis' > 'Start Smart Hub Connection Test'
  4. If either test fails, have the subscriber restart their TV and router, then retest

Fire TV Devices

  1. From the Home screen, go to 'Settings' > 'Network'
  2. Highlight the connected network and press the 'Play/Pause' button
  3. View the Signal Strength reading (should be 'Good' or 'Very Good')

Fire TV and Apple TV can run full-speed tests using an internet browser app if installed.

Fetch 

  1. Network testing is handled through the Fetch connection menu
  2. Press 'Menu' > 'Manage' > 'Settings' > 'Network' > 'Connection Test'
  3. Wait for results and confirm signal strength and connectivity

If results show low speed or poor signal strength, recommend restarting the router and reconnecting the device before trying playback again.

Uninstalling and Reinstalling the BritBox App

Uninstalling and reinstalling the BritBox app can resolve issues caused by corrupted files, outdated data, or incomplete updates. This process ensures a clean installation and refreshes the app’s connection to the device.

Below are the steps to uninstall and reinstall the BritBox app, depending on the device.

Instructions:

Android Devices

Method 1 – Through Settings

  1. Open the Settings app
  2. Tap 'Apps' or 'Apps & notifications'
  3. Find and select BritBox
  4. Tap 'Uninstall,' then confirm
  5. Restart the device before reinstalling the app from the Google Play Store

Method 2 – From the App Icon (Android 7 or newer)

  1. Long-press the BritBox app icon
  2. Select 'App info'
  3. Tap 'Uninstall,' then confirm
  4. Restart the device before reinstalling from the Google Play Store

Clear the cache and data before uninstalling to remove stored files completely.

Apple iPhone and iPad

  1. Find the BritBox app on the Home screen
  2. Long-press the app icon until options appear
  3. Tap 'Delete App,' then confirm
  4. On older iOS versions, tap the 'X' in the corner of the app icon
  5. Restart the device before reinstalling from the App Store

Roku Devices

  1. Press the 'Home' button on the Roku remote
  2. Highlight the BritBox channel
  3. Press the 'Star (*)' button to open options
  4. Select 'Remove Channel,' then confirm
  5. Restart the Roku from 'Settings' > 'System' > 'System Restart'
  6. Reinstall BritBox from the Roku Channel Store

If the user has a Roku-billed BritBox subscription, they must cancel the subscription before removing the channel.

Fire TV Devices

  1. From the Home screen, go to 'Settings' > 'Applications' > 'Manage Installed Applications'
  2. Select BritBox
  3. Choose 'Uninstall,' then confirm
  4. Restart the device and reinstall BritBox from the Amazon Appstore

Apple TV

  1. Highlight the BritBox app on the Home screen
  2. Press and hold the Touch surface on the remote until the icons start to jiggle
  3. Press the 'Play/Pause' button and select 'Delete'
  4. Confirm, then reinstall BritBox from the App Store

Samsung Smart TVs

  1. Press the 'Home' button on the Samsung remote
  2. Navigate to 'Apps,' then select the Settings icon in the top-right corner
  3. Highlight BritBox
  4. Select 'Delete' to uninstall, or 'Reinstall' to refresh the app

If Reinstall doesn’t resolve the issue, uninstall the app completely, restart the TV,and reinstall it from the app store.

LG Smart TVs

  1. Press the 'Home' button on the LG remote
  2. Navigate to the BritBox app
  3. Press and hold the 'OK/Select' button until the icons enter edit mode
  4. Press the 'Up' button and select the 'X' to delete the app
  5. Confirm and reinstall BritBox from the LG Content Store

Fetch

  1. Press 'Menu' > 'Manage' > 'Apps'
  2. Find and select BritBox
  3. Select 'Uninstall,' then confirm
  4. Restart the Fetch box before reinstalling from the Fetch App Store

After reinstalling, confirm the subscriber can sign in and access their watchlist. If login or playback errors continue, advise clearing app cache or rebooting the device.

Information to Gather After Troubleshooting

When troubleshooting does not resolve an issue and it needs to be escalated to HCL, certain information must be collected first. These details help HCL quickly identify, reproduce, and resolve the problem without delays or unnecessary back-and-forth.

The required information differs depending on the type of issue: Billing, Content, or Technical.

Billing Escalations

Required Data Fields:

  • Account Email Address
  • CIP (Customer Identification Profile – use N/A if not applicable)
  • Entitlement Removal Request (Yes / No / N/A)
  • Customer Country
  • Description of Issue (include error messages if visible)
  • Frequency of Issue (One-time / Recurring / N/A)
  • Date and Time Issue Started (use N/A if unknown)
  • Additional Information (relevant screenshots, notes or test results)

Macro to use: Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Billing

Content Escalations

Required Data Fields:

  • Device (type and brand)
  • Device Characteristics (model and operating system)
  • Device App Version
  • BritBox App Version
  • Browser (if web playback issue)
  • Affected Content (specific shows, films, or episodes)
  • Customer Country
  • Description of Issue (include what the user expected vs. what occurred)
  • Frequency of Issue
  • Has the Issue Been Replicated? (Tested and replicated/Tested but could not replicate/Unable to test or replicate)
  • Date and Time Issue Started
  • Time Playback Issue Starts (if applicable)
  • Additional Information (include screenshots, URLs or test results)

Macro to use: Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Content

Tech Escalations

Required Data Fields:

  • Device (type and brand)
  • Device Characteristics:
    • Model
    • Year Model
    • Operating System
  • Device App Version (if applicable)
  • BritBox App Version
  • Browser (if applicable)
  • IP Address
  • VPN Use (Y/N)
  • Customer Country
  • Description of Issue
  • Does the Issue Occur When Opening the App or During Playback?
  • Frequency of Issue (One-time / Recurring / N/A)
  • Has the Issue Been Replicated? (Tested and replicated/Tested but could not replicate/Unable to test or replicate)
  • Actions Taken:
  • Device Reboot (Y/N)
  • Uninstall and Reinstall (Y/N)
  • App Update (Y/N)
  • OS Update (Y/N)
  • VPN Disabled (Y/N)
  • Date and Time Issue Started
  • Additional Information (screenshots, test results, or error codes)

Macro to use: Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Tech

Before escalating, review all related tickets and internal notes to check whether the required data has already been collected. Use existing information wherever possible instead of asking the subscriber to repeat details unnecessarily. Missing information can delay HCL’s response or cause the ticket to be returned for more details.
