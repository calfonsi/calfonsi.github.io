This article lists all our messaging shortcuts, with descriptions and guidance on how to use them when replying to subscribers in live messaging conversation.

Introduction

Shortcuts are essential for maintaining consistency and efficiency in every subscriber messaging interaction. They provide agents with pre-approved quick replies that save time and help keep our brand voice consistent across conversations. When used correctly, shortcuts make it easier to respond quickly, reduce typing errors, and create a smoother experience for subscribers. Understanding their approval status and usage guidelines ensures agents use shortcuts appropriately and provide support that is both fast and accurate.

Usage

Shortcuts are approved for use only in messaging interactions. They should not be used for email or social media, as each channel has its own approved tools and guidelines. For those channels, check out our Macros and Assets articles for more details.

Guidelines

  • Avoid Stacking: Do not paste multiple shortcuts in a row. Combine or reword them so the conversation flows smoothly.
  • Keep It Conversational: Messaging replies should be short and natural, not long blocks of text that feel like email.
  • Mandatory Use: If a shortcut exists that answers the customer’s question or provides a starting point, it must be used.
  • Region-Specific Shortcuts: If the title includes a country code (e.g., US), only use it for subscribers in that region.
  • Unnecessary Edits: Do not make changes based on personal style or preference.
  • Use Empathetic Tone: Always check that the wording sounds friendly, clear, and human.

Customizing

Shortcuts should be adjusted as needed to fully resolve the customer’s issue. This may involve:

  • Avoid Jargon: Use simple, clear language that any subscriber can understand.
  • Combining Shortcuts: Use more than one shortcut together if it helps provide a complete response.
  • Keep It Short: Messaging replies should be concise and easy to read; avoid long blocks of text.
  • Linking FAQs: If a shortcut does not fully resolve the issue, always link to the relevant FAQ when an article exists.
  • Subscriber Personalization: Add the customer’s name or small touches that make the reply feel human without straying from the approved message.

Quality Considerations

  • Deviations: Any deviation from these guidelines may result in markdowns under Resolution Communication.
  • Freeform Responses: Allowed only when:
    • No existing shortcut covers the request.
    • An existing shortcut cannot be edited to fit the situation.
    • Two or more shortcuts cannot be merged to provide a complete response.
  • Improper Edits: Personal edits (such as changes for tone, preference, or unnecessary personalization) will be marked down, even if grammatically correct.
  • Shortcut Accuracy: Shortcuts should not be altered unless necessary to accurately respond to the request.
