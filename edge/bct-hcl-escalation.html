BCT and HCL Escalation Workflows

This article explains how to escalate a ticket to HCL for research or process.

Overview

HCL supports a wide range of ticket types for Customer Care, including BCT troubleshooting escalations, certain billing process requests, and account erasures or data deletion cases. Examples of escalation types include:

  • Technical issues (new or previously reported)
  • App crashes, freezing, or persistent buffering
  • Login failures not resolved through basic troubleshooting
  • Promo codes not applying
  • Content-related errors or discrepancies
  • Missing episodes or incorrect availability
  • Entitlement removal requests
  • Account whitelisting
  • Billing errors or access issues
  • Subscription not activating after payment
  • Backend data mismatches
  • Account erasures or data deletion requests
  • Non-device related podcast issues with the content delivery

BCT Issue Escalations

When a subscriber reports an issue to Customer Care, the first step is to identify the correct issue type (Billing, Content, or Tech) so you can determine the appropriate next actions.

  • Billing: Issues related to payment processing, refund errors, entitlement access, whitelisting, or billing failures.
  • Content: Missing episodes, incorrect availability, wrong-title playback, or other content-related errors.
  • Tech: Problems affecting playback, app performance, logins, or streaming functionality.

Once the issue type is confirmed, Customer Care should complete three core tasks in their investigation:

  • Perform basic troubleshooting steps.
  • Test or replicate the issue when possible. If testing is not possible, collect screenshots or video from the subscriber.
  • Collect key information from the subscriber, including ruling out potential blockers (e.g., a VPN for tech issues or a chargeback for billing issues).

All must be completed fully before escalating the ticket further.

⚠️ Attention: The BCT articles (Escalation Workflows, Data Gathering Guide, Troubleshooting Steps, and Testing) do not follow a fixed sequence. Customer Care should start with the article that best fits the situation, as the order may change based on the subscriber’s concern. These resources work together and should be applied thoughtfully for each case.

Check out our BCT Data Gathering Guide, BCT Testing (Issue Replication), and BCT Troubleshooting Steps articles for more information. 

How to Escalate to HCL

Below are the instructions for raising a ticket to HCL for issue troubleshooting.

Instructions:
  1. For messaging, create a child ticket by clicking the plus sign in 'Side Conversation' to generate a new email ticket. Email can skip to Step 4
  2. In the 'Internal Note' of the new ticket, enter: “BritBox Care Ticket #NNNNNN” using the newly created ticket number. This becomes the subject line of the ticket
  3. Click 'Submit'
  4. Use the tool that matches the issue type: Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Billing, Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Content, Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Tech or Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Podcast to raise the ticket to HCL
  5. Fill out all details in the form
  6. Fields not showing N/A as an option are required to submit, do not use N/A if the field will help in processing the request
  7. Save ticket 'On Hold'
  8. Keep the ticket assigned to yourself unless told to reassign

Next Steps After Escalation

Customer Care keeps tickets open only long enough to gather any additional information HCL may need, address any remaining subscriber questions, or complete required follow-ups when an issue needs subscriber's action after a fix is completed (some billing issues as example).

⚠️ Attention: When a ticket is under investigation, do not “bump” or request an update from HCL unless the SLA is significantly outside the expected timeframe for that issue type. If all required data has been collected and no other issues are pending, a Team Lead may send an internal note to HCL asking whether they require any additional information from the subscriber or if the ticket can be closed.

We do not keep tickets open for the full duration of the Billing, Content, or Tech investigation. The reason is that issue fix timelines vary based on factors, such as:

  • The priority of the issue with our product, tech, or billing teams
  • Research and processing time needed by BritBox teams
  • Work required from supporting vendors, such as Samsung TV, Apple, or even Evergent
  • Whether the fix must be included in an app update

Once it's been determined that HCL has all the information needed from the subscriber, we will solve the ticket using the most appropriate tool for the request. These generally fall into four categories:

HQ1 – Known Issue, Under Investigation

The issue has been reported and is still being investigated. No fix is available yet.

Tool to Use: Troubleshooting :: Tech Bug HQ1

Actions:

  • Add the applicable HCL-provided tag to the ticket.
  • Encourage the subscriber to use BritBox on another device if the issue is preventing core functions.
  • Mark the ticket Solved.

HQ2 – Cause Identified, Fix in Progress

HCL has identified the root cause, and the engineering team is working on a fix.

Tool to Use: Troubleshooting :: Tech Bug HQ2

Actions:

  • Add the applicable HCL-provided tag to the ticket.
  • Encourage the subscriber to use BritBox on another device if the issue is preventing core functions.
  • Provide guidance on how to update the app or confirm that auto-updates are enabled when applicable.
  • Mark the ticket Solved.

HQ3 – Fix Completed, Pending Release

HCL has completed the fix, but it has not yet been released publicly.

Tool to Use: Troubleshooting :: Tech Bug HQ3

Actions:

  • Add the applicable HCL-provided tag to the ticket.
  • Encourage the subscriber to use BritBox on another device if the issue is preventing core functions.
  • Explain how to update their specific app or confirm auto-updates when applicable..
  • Mark the ticket Solved and allow it to auto-close if needed.

Fix Released (Issue Resolved)

The fix has been released and the known issue is resolved.

Tool to Use: Troubleshooting :: General App Fix-Refresh

Actions:

  • Add the applicable HCL-provided tag.
  • Provide steps on how to update the app or confirm auto-updates.
  • Mark the ticket Solved and allow it to auto-close.

Some subscribers may ask us to keep their Zendesk ticket open during the research and discovery phase. Since we cannot provide timelines for most fixes, we need to manage these requests carefully.

Use the follow-up tools below to show empathy while still following our policy not to keep tickets open unless further subscriber engagement is required.

  • Troubleshooting :: Cannot Keep Ticket Open Explanation
  • Troubleshooting :: Error/Issue Cause Unknown
  • Troubleshooting :: Escalated Issue Fixed Pending App Update

⚠️ Attention: If it involves an escalated subscriber demanding to keep the ticket open, the ticket may remain on hold only with documented approval from BritBox leadership in an internal note.

When We Cannot Find or Replicate an Issue

In some cases, after a ticket is escalated and HCL has completed the investigation, the reported issue cannot be replicated. This usually happens when:

  • The error does not reoccur during testing
  • The issue was caused by temporary streaming or network conditions outside BritBox’s control
  • Device or environment variables (WiFi interference, device state, temporary data corruption, etc.) cannot be reproduced
  • Playback or app logs show no errors at the time the subscriber experienced the problem

When this occurs, we send a clear, empathetic response to the subscriber. This ensures they understand that:

  • Their issue was taken seriously
  • Our technical teams ran the error through multiple tests
  • We were unable to reproduce the issue despite several attempts
  • The root cause cannot be confidently identified due to a lack of reproducible data
  • This outcome is rare, but can happen with streaming services

If a refund or goodwill adjustment is appropriate, Customer Care can include that information at the designated point in the tool. Otherwise, reassure the subscriber, close the ticket, and encourage them to reach out again if the issue returns.

This approach provides transparency, maintains trust, and avoids keeping a ticket open when no actionable fix exists.

Other HCL Escalations

Erasure and Data Deletion 

HCL also manages account erasure and data deletion requests. Check out our Account and Credit Card Erasure article for more details.

They additionally support entitlement-removal requests, used to turn off access to the service when required. Below are the instructions on how to request and escalate for entitlement removal.

Instructions:
  1. Use the Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Billing tool to raise the ticket to HCL
  2. Fill out all details in the form
  3. Fields not showing N/A as an option are required to submit, do not use N/A if the field will help in processing the request
  4. Save ticket 'On Hold'
  5. Keep the ticket assigned to yourself unless told to reassign
  6. Check the ticket regularly and wait for HCL’s completion note, they’ll confirm entitlement removal in an internal note
  7. Close the ticket

On The Box Podcast

Use this process only for On The Box: The BritBox Podcast when an issue is not related to a subscriber’s device or app settings. 

Escalate if you can replicate the issue, if an episode is missing across multiple platforms, if the audio appears corrupted, if incorrect content is playing, or if the issue seems widespread. Customer Care must:

  • Attempt to replicate the issue on the same platform.
  • Confirm whether the issue appears platform-wide.
  • Capture the required details listed below.

If you cannot replicate the issue and it appears device-related, direct the subscriber to platform support instead.

Include the following in your escalation using the Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Podcast tool:

  • Episode title
  • Platform (Apple Podcasts, Spotify, YouTube, etc.)
  • Subscriber’s country
  • Description of the issue
  • Confirmation that replication was attempted
  • Screenshots or screen recordings if available

Incomplete information in the ticket may delay the investigation. Once HCL has confirmed receipt, you may inform the subscriber that the issue has been reported and resolve the inquiry. We cannot guarantee resolution timelines, and these tickets should not be kept open.
