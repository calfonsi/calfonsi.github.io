BCT Data Gathering Guide

Use this handy guide to understand the information to gather from subscribers before escalating for Billing, Content, or Tech (BCT) research.

Introduction

When a subscriber reports an issue to Customer Care, the first step is to identify the correct issue type (Billing, Content, or Tech) so you can determine the appropriate next actions.

  • Billing: Issues related to payment processing, refund errors, entitlement access, whitelisting, or billing failures.
  • Content: Missing episodes, incorrect availability, wrong-title playback, or other content-related errors.
  • Tech: Problems affecting playback, app performance, logins, or streaming functionality.

Once the issue type is confirmed, Customer Care should complete three core tasks in their investigation:

  • Perform basic troubleshooting steps.
  • Test or replicate the issue when possible. If testing is not possible, collect screenshots or video from the subscriber.
  • Collect key information from the subscriber, including ruling out potential blockers (e.g., a VPN for tech issues or a chargeback for billing issues).

All must be completed fully before escalating the ticket further.

As noted above, it’s important to gather all required information. Having complete and accurate details helps our technical teams diagnose issues quickly, reduces back-and-forth, and prevents delays.

⚠️ Attention: The BCT articles (Escalation Workflows, Data Gathering Guide, Troubleshooting Steps, and Testing) do not follow a fixed sequence. Customer Care should start with the article that best fits the situation, as the order may change based on the subscriber’s concern. These resources work together and should be applied thoughtfully for each case.

Before You Begin

Before you send any data request to the subscriber, take a moment to review what we already have. This helps avoid asking the customer for details they’ve already provided and prevents repeat work for both the agent and the subscriber. Tips for review:

  • All related tickets connected to the subscriber, including older cases about the same issue.
  • The subscribers replies to see what has already been shared.
  • Internal notes from previous agents, including test results, screenshots, or troubleshooting steps.
  • Billing or entitlement history (for Billing issues) to check for notes about previous errors, chargebacks, or entitlements.
  • Playback or device history (for Content/Tech issues) if the customer has had similar problems before.

How to use existing information:

  • If required fields have already been captured, copy that information into the escalation form or your internal notes.
  • Only reach out to the subscriber if the information is missing, outdate,  or unclear.
  • If previous details conflict (for example, two different devices are listed), verify with the subscriber.

Billing Questions

These are examples of questions to ask when the issue involves charges, refunds, payments, access, or entitlements.

Account and Access

  • What email address is linked to your BritBox account?
  • Are you requesting that we remove your entitlement or cancel your access?

Issue Details

  • Can you describe the issue you’re experiencing?
  • Are you seeing any error messages? If so, what do they say?
  • When did this issue first start?
  • Has this happened more than once, or is it the first time?

Additional Information

  • Can you share any screenshots of the error or billing issue?
  • Have you recently updated your payment method or received any alerts from your bank?
  • Are you based in <customer country>?

Content Questions

These are examples of questions to ask when specific shows, episodes, or availability issues are involved.

Device and Environment

  • What device are you using to watch BritBox?
  • What is the model and operating system of that device?
  • What version of the BritBox app are you using?
  • If you’re watching on a web browser, which browser are you using?

Content Details

  • Which show, film, season, or episode is affected?
  • What were you expecting to happen?
  • What actually happened instead?

Playback and Replication

  • Does the issue happen every time, or only sometimes?
  • Were you able to test the issue again?
  • Did it happen again?
  • If this is a playback issue, does it happen at a specific time in the video?
  • If so, at what timestamp?

Additional Information

  • Can you share screenshots, URLs or recordings of what you’re seeing?
  • When did this issue first start?

Tech Questions

These are examples of questions to ask when the issue involves streaming problems, app crashes, login errors, device performance, or connectivity.

Device Information

  • What device are you using (brand and model)?
  • What is the model year of the device?
  • What operating system version is installed?
  • What version of the BritBox app are you using?
  • If using a browser, could you share your browser information via whatsmybrowser.com?

Network and Access

  • Are you or your internet provider using a VPN?
  • What is your IP address?
  • What is your internet speed? (Please run a test at fast.com.)

⚠️ Attention: When collecting an IP address, always ask for an IPv4 address. Subscribers may sometimes share an IPv6 address by mistake, which cannot be used in most investigations. You can recognize IPv4 as a short number format (for example, 159.196.248.219), while IPv6 is longer and includes letters and colons (for example, fe80::c96a:346:2d24:1cb7). If a subscriber shares an IPv6 guide them to get the correct IPv4 by visiting: https://whatismyipaddress.com/ from the affected device’s browser.

Issue Details

  • Can you describe what’s happening when the issue occurs?
  • Does the issue happen when you open the app or during playback?
  • How often does this happen — once, or repeatedly?
  • When did this issue first start?

Troubleshooting Attempts

  • Have you tried restarting your device?
  • Have you reinstalled the BritBox app?
  • Has the device or app been updated recently?
  • If you use a VPN, have you tried turning it off?

Additional Information

  • Can you share any screenshots, error codes or recordings?
  • Does the issue happen on multiple devices or only one?

Submission

Once all basic troubleshooting, attempted internal replication (where applicable), and required data gathering are completed, it’s time to send the case to HCL for further investigation. 

Check out our BCT and HCL Escalation Workflows for more details on escalating. Here’s what each submission must include.

⚠️ Attention: Do not leave any sections blank. If a field is left blank, the ticket will be returned, marked as a markdown, or coaching opportunity. Use 'N/A' where applicable, but using 'N/A' when a research response is required will also result in a returned ticket, markdown, or coaching opportunity.

The required information differs depending on the type of issue: Billing, Content, or Technical.

Billing Escalations

Required Data Fields:

  • Account Email Address
  • CIP (Customer Identification Profile – use N/A if not applicable)
  • Entitlement Removal Request (Yes / No / N/A)
  • Customer Country
  • Description of Issue (include error messages if visible)
  • Frequency of Issue (One-time / Recurring / N/A)
  • Date and Time Issue Started (use N/A if unknown)
  • Additional Information (relevant screenshots, notes or test results)

Macro to use: Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Billing

Content Escalations

Required Data Fields:

  • Device (type and brand)
  • Device Characteristics (model and operating system)
  • Device App Version
  • BritBox App Version
  • Browser (if web playback issue)
  • Affected Content (specific shows, films, or episodes)
  • Customer Country
  • Description of Issue (include what the user expected vs. what occurred)
  • Frequency of Issue
  • Has the Issue Been Replicated? (Tested and replicated/Tested but could not replicate/Unable to test or replicate)
  • Date and Time Issue Started
  • Time Playback Issue Starts (if applicable)
  • Additional Information (include screenshots, URLs or test results)

Macro to use: Internal Use :: Escalation HCL Content

Tech Escalations

Required Data Fields:

  • Device (type and brand)
  • Device Characteristics:
    • Model
    • Year Model
    • Operating System
  • Device App Version (if applicable)
  • BritBox App Version
  • Browser (if applicable)
  • IP Address
  • VPN Use (Y/N)
  • Customer Country
  • Description of Issue
  • Does the Issue Occur When Opening the App or During Playback?
  • Frequency of Issue (One-time / Recurring / N/A)
  • Has the Issue Been Replicated? (Tested and replicated/Tested but could not replicate/Unable to test or replicate)
  • Actions Taken:
  • Device Reboot (Y/N)
  • Uninstall and Reinstall (Y/N)
  • App Update (Y/N)
  • OS Update (Y/N)
  • VPN Disabled (Y/N)
  • Date and Time Issue Started
  • Additional Information (screenshots, test results, or error codes)
