Assets

This article lists all our social media assets, with descriptions and helpful information on how to use them to engage subscribers across our social platforms.

Introduction

Assets help keep our social media replies consistent and efficient. Unlike macros or shortcuts, assets are not strict scripts. Instead, they give a starting point for wording. Since social media works best with a friendly, natural tone, the Social Media Team should feel free to adjust assets as needed so responses sound genuine and engaging.

Usage

Assets are approved for use on social media, including direct messages and public posts. They should not be used for email or messaging. For those channels, check out our Macros and Shortcuts articles for more details. 

Guidelines

  • Avoid Repetition in Threads: Don’t post the same asset wording multiple times in one conversation. Repeating can feel robotic or dismissive.
  • Personalize for Tone: Adjust wording so replies feel natural, friendly, and human. Socials should sound like a real conversation, not a script.
  • Public vs. Private: Use assets carefully in public posts to keep responses short, clear, and brand-safe. For sensitive issues, guide the subscriber into a private message.
  • Region-Specific Assets: If the title includes a country code (e.g., US), only use it for persons in that region.
  • Suggested Use: If an asset exists that answers the question or can serve as a starting point, use it—unless it’s already been used in the same thread or very recently in a similar conversation.

Customizing

  • Be Mindful of Visibility: Public replies are seen by all followers, so ensure accuracy, professionalism, and brand consistency. If sensitive details are needed, guide the subscriber into a private message.
  • Brevity Wins: Social media works best with short, easy-to-read replies. Trim long sentences or break them into two.
  • Combining Assets: Use more than one asset together if it helps give a complete response.
  • Keep it On-Brand: Edit for clarity, but make sure the reply still sounds like BritBox, not personal opinion.
  • Link Smartly: Add Help Center links only when they genuinely solve the issue; avoid spamming links in every reply.
  • Rewording Responses: Change the wording so the reply keeps a friendly and engaging tone.
  • Stay Public-Safe: When posting publicly, avoid adding account details or troubleshooting steps that belong in a private message.
  • Tailoring Text: Remove or add details so the response matches the specific concern.
  • Use Concise Language: Keep replies short and clear. 
  • Use Emojis Appropriately: Emojis can make responses engaging, but meanings differ by region. If unsure, leave them out.
