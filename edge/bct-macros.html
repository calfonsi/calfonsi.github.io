This article lists all our macros, with descriptions and guidance on how to use them when replying to subscribers by email. It also explains how to use macros to escalate or move tickets from one workstream to another.

Introduction

Macros are essential for maintaining consistency and efficiency in customer email interactions. They give agents pre-approved wording that saves time, reduces mistakes, and keeps our brand voice professional. Using macros correctly ensures subscribers receive accurate and timely responses, no matter which agent handles their case.

Understanding approval status and usage guidelines is important, since not all macros are approved for every situation. By following these rules, Customer Care can streamline support, avoid errors, and deliver a smoother experience for subscribers.

Usage

Macros are approved for use only in email interactions. They should not be used for messaging or social media, as each channel has its own approved tools and guidelines. For those channels, check out our Shortcuts and Assets articles for more details.

Guidelines

  • Editing Guidelines: Only edit the sections marked as <INDICATED TEXT> or when the subscriber's email requires changes to make the macro fit. Do not make unnecessary edits based on personal style preferences.
  • Generic Email Inserts: Always include these in freeform responses; they are required for consistency.
  • Mandatory Use: If a macro exists that answers the subscriber's question or provides a good starting point, it must be used.
  • Region-Specific Macros:  If the title includes a country code (e.g., US), only use it for subscribers in that region.

Customizing

  • Address the Concern: Make sure the response directly answers the question without adding unnecessary detail.
  • Aim for FCR (First Contact Resolution): Try to resolve all concerns in the first reply to avoid back-and-forth.
  • Avoid Formatting Errors: Don’t add extra spaces or line breaks that create gaps in the email.
  • Combining Macros: Use more than one macro together if it helps provide a complete response.
  • Keep Salutations Consistent: Do not modify the greeting or closing; leave them as written.
  • Linking FAQs: If a macro does not fully resolve the issue, always link to the relevant FAQ when an article exists.
  • Personalize Lightly: Small edits (like acknowledging the exact issue) make the reply feel human without straying from the approved message.
  • Stay On-Brand: Keep the wording professional, empathetic, and in BritBox’s voice.
  • Tailoring Text: Remove or add wording so the message directly aligns with the concern.
  • Watch for Duplicates: If more than one macro could work, pick the clearest one; don’t stack multiple macros that repeat the same point.

⚠️ Attention: Agents are permitted to use the Enhance Writing tool in Zendesk when drafting responses; however, they should click it only once, as repeated use may alter the intended message.

Quality Considerations

  • Deviations: Any deviation from these guidelines may result in markdowns under Resolution Communication.
  • Freeform Responses: Allowed only when:
    • No existing macro covers the request.
    • An existing macro cannot be edited to fit the situation.
    • Two or more macros cannot be merged to provide a complete response.
  • Improper Edits: Personal edits (such as changes for tone, preference, or unnecessary personalization) will be marked down, even if grammatically correct.
  • Macro Accuracy: Macros should not be altered unless necessary to accurately respond to the request.
